ESG Glossary

A - E

  • Article 6, 8 & 9 funds - Classification under the EU’s Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation (SFDR) framework for disclosure of investment funds’ sustainability characteristics
  • Biodiversity - Biodiversity is all the different kinds of life you’ll find in one area—the variety of animals, plants, fungi, and even microorganisms like bacteria that make up our natural world (WWF)
  • Carbon footprint - A measure of the total greenhouse gas emissions produced by an individual, group, or entity
  • Carbon intensity - The carbon intensity of electricity is the number of grams of carbon dioxide (CO2) it takes to make one unit of electricity at one kilowatt per hour (kW/hour)
  • Carbon neutrality - Carbon neutrality means having a balance between emitting carbon and absorbing carbon from the atmosphere in carbon sinks (EU)
  • Circular economy - The circular economy is a model of production and consumption, which involves sharing, leasing, reusing, repairing, refurbishing and recycling existing materials and products as long as possible (EU)
  • Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) - a management concept whereby companies integrate social and environmental concerns in their business operations and interactions with their stakeholders (UN)
  • ESG - Environmental Social Governance
  • ESG Controversies - When a company faces (negative) reputational risk because of ESG-related issues
  • EU TAXONOMY - a classification system established to clarify which investments are environmentally sustainable, in the context of the European Green Deal

F - P

  • MiFID II - Markets in Financial Instruments Directive, MiFID II is a legislative framework instituted by the European Union to regulate financial markets in the bloc and improve protections for investors.
  • Greenhouse gases (GHG) - Gases that trap heat in the atmosphere, including carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide, and the fluorinated gases
  • Greenwashing - Using marketing or misleading information to lead stakeholders to believe in its green image
  • Global Reporting Initiative (GRI) - an international independent standards organization
  • Human rights - Human rights are rights inherent to all human beings, regardless of race, sex, nationality, ethnicity, language, religion, or any other status (UN)
  • Net Zero - cutting greenhouse gas emissions to as close to zero as possible (UN)
  • Principal Adverse Impacts (PAI)
  • Principles for Responsible Investment (PRI) - is the world’s leading proponent of responsible investment

Q - Z

  • SASB - Sustainability Accounting Standards Board, a guide to the disclosure of financially material sustainability information by companies to their investors
  • Science-Based Targets Initiative (SBTi) - defines and promotes best practices in science-based target setting
  • Scope 1 emissions - direct greenhouse (GHG) emissions that occur from sources that are controlled or owned by an organization
  • Scope 2 emissions - indirect GHG emissions associated with the purchase of electricity, steam, heat, or cooling
  • Scope 3 emissions - the result of activities from assets not owned or controlled by the reporting organization, but that the organization indirectly impacts in its value chain
  • SFDR - Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation
  • TCFD - The Task Force on Climate-Related Financial Disclosures (TCFD) develops consistent climate-related financial risk disclosures for use by companies, banks, and investors in providing information to stakeholders
  • UN SDG - The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a collection of 17 interlinked global goals designed to be a "blueprint to achieve a better and more sustainable future for all"

Automated EU Taxonomy and SFDR reports

The recent EU Taxonomy regulation defines criteria to assess whether a given economic activity is to be considered as environmentally sustainable or not. The taxonomy itself is made of 88 activities that have been defined by the European legislator. To be considered as EU Taxonomic eligible, a company must conduct at least an economic activity present in this list.

Companies’ alignment to EU Taxonomy is now a crucial consideration when it comes to its ESG performance assessment. Our technology can assess and align any company to the EU Taxonomy and perform eligibility checks in seconds. In addition, EU Taxonomy alignment is now required to be disclosed by financial institutions when publishing mandatory extra-financial reports such as SFDR. Estimation Engine with continuous updates

The EU Taxonomy is completed by Delegated Acts released by the European Commission. Not all the Delegated Acts have been published as of today which means some areas cannot be assessed in theory, such as waste management, circular economy, pollution prevention and protection/restoration of biodiversity and ecosystems.

However our FINGREEN ENGINE can estimate the compliance and alignment of every company to those objectives based on ingestion of hundreds of other standards and practices.
